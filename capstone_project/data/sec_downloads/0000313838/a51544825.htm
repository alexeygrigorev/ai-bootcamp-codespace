All amounts are presented on the basis of Generally Accepted Accounting Principles in the U.S. (“U.S. GAAP”).

The forecast for consolidated results for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2017, as announced on February 2, 2017, was based on assumed foreign currency exchange rates for the fourth quarter (from January 1, 2017 to March 31, 2017) of approximately 118 yen to one U.S. dollar and approximately 123 yen to one euro.  The average rates for the fourth quarter were 113.7 yen to one U.S. dollar and 121.1 yen to one euro.

The forecast for sales and operating revenue remains unchanged from the February forecast.

The forecast for consolidated operating income has been revised upward due to expected improvement, compared with the February forecast, in all segments other than the Components segment, which is expected to deteriorate compared with the February forecast.  The primary reasons for the upward revision in the segments that are anticipated to improve are expected decreases in amortization of deferred insurance acquisition costs and other costs in the Financial Services segment and lower costs than anticipated in February in the other segments, particularly Semiconductors.

The forecasts for income before income taxes and net income attributable to Sony Corporation’s stockholders have been similarly revised upward primarily due to the above-mentioned upward revision in consolidated operating income.

The current forecast has been prepared based on information available at the time of the issuance of this release.  Actual results may differ from this forecast due to a variety of factors.  See “Cautionary Statement” below for further details.

The actual consolidated results for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2017 and the consolidated results forecast for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2018 are scheduled to be announced on April 28, 2017.
